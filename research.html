My Research

CFD Simulation of a stalled NACA 23012 Airfoil

Beginning in April, 2018, I began working on a research project under Professor Lakshmi Sankar. In the project, I worked on CFD verification, comparing CFD results with published results collected from a wind tunnel. In particular, I focued on a NACA 23012 airfoil in Fall 2018. On the left is a velocity contour plot for a NACA 23012 airfoil at an angle of attack of 6 degrees.

As the plot on the right shows, the technique researched during this process produced accurate data to within a 5-10% margin of error. Another aspect of the research that this plot cannot show is the computation time savings as the result of this process. To produce these results, a Dell XPS-15 workstation with 8 cores, 16 GB of ram, and an internal 2.2-GHz Intel Core i7-8750H processor was used. Total computation time for the 2D airfoil totaled only 5 minutes on average, over 1000 iterations of calculations.

Various methods were used to create the meshes used in the analysis. To learn more about this project, please feel free to download the report I wrote.

My current research project has focued on extending the current 2D CFD method to 3D models. More coming soon!
