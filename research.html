My Research

Goal

Create a wholistic approach to the CFD process that provides accurate data while minimizing computation power and time.

Method

Building off of previous techniques, generate meshes that are dense in important areas and sparse in unimporant areas of the model

Results

The CFD process produced accurate data to within a 5-10% margin of error. Using a Dell XPS-15 workstation with 8 cores, 16 GB of ram and 2.2-GHz Intel Core i7-8750H processor, the computation converged in only 5 minutes on average, taking less than 1000 iterations of calculations.

Various methods were used to create the meshes used in the analysis. To learn more about this project, please feel free to download the report I wrote:

Method

Modification of current techniques and trial and error.

Testing

The technique was tested against NASA CFD data for the Onera M6 Wing, and against Lockheed Martin data for the LANN Wing

Goal

Extrapolate 2D CFD method to complex, 3D models

Results

Qualitatively, the results closely matched the NASA CFD results (see pressure contours).
Quantitatively, the results proved the CFD method described in the report was accurate (see pressure coefficient plots below).
The method also proved to be less computation-heavy in comparison to other methods, while still maintaining a high degree of accuracy.
